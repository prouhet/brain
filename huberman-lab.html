"What does Huberman say about combining cold exposure and exercise — and how does this affect dopamine and sleep?"
Never cold immediately after strength training. Cold within 4 hours of a hypertrophy session blocks the inflammatory response your muscles need to grow — do it before training or skip it that day.

Cold between sets is a different story. Palmer cooling between exercise sets increased pull-up volume by 144% over 6 weeks. Muscle contraction enzymes only work in a narrow temperature range — strategic cooling during cardio prevents overheating without killing adaptation signals.

The dopamine effect is outsized. Cold exposure raises dopamine 250% above baseline and holds it there for up to 3 hours. Combined with exercise (which also releases dopamine), the neurochemical state is powerful.

Timing protects your sleep. Cold late in the day raises core temperature when it needs to drop 1–3°F to initiate sleep. Keep cold exposure to the morning or early afternoon.
