Adding Videos
Three ways to get any YouTube video into your library
reelcall.app/reel.html
Paste a YouTube video or channel URL...
Paste & Analyze
📋
📋
Paste & Analyze Button
Tap Paste & Analyze — it reads your clipboard and immediately starts analyzing the video. No typing needed. On mobile this is the fastest way.
🔗
Paste URL Manually
Click the URL bar, paste any YouTube link, and tap Analyze or press Enter. Tracking parameters like ?si=xxx are stripped automatically.
📤
Share from YouTube App
In the YouTube app, tap the Share icon (or ···) → Copy link, then paste in ReelCall. On iPhone you can Share → ReelCall directly if it's on your home screen.
💡
From a YouTube video page in your browser: Tap the three dots ··· or Share icon → Copy link → return to ReelCall → tap Paste & Analyze. Done in seconds.
1
Find a video on YouTube
On mobile, open the YouTube app or browser. Find the video you want to capture. You can add any public YouTube video — lectures, podcasts, tutorials, interviews, anything.
2
Copy the link
Tap the Share button under the video → Copy link. On desktop, copy the URL from your browser's address bar.
📱 iPhone tip: Tap the share icon (box with up arrow) → Copy Link
3
Tap Paste & Analyze in ReelCall
Switch to ReelCall and tap the blue Paste & Analyze button. It reads your clipboard and starts immediately. Analysis takes 20–60 seconds depending on video length.
4
It appears in your library
When done, the video is automatically saved to your library with a summary, key points, quotes, suggested actions, and credibility assessment. It's ready to ask questions immediately.
⚡ If the video is already in the system from another user — analysis is instant. No wait.
📺
Import an Entire Channel
Paste a channel URL like youtube.com/@ChannelName or @ChannelName directly. The button changes to Browse Channel — tap it to see recent videos and pick which ones to import in bulk.
📋
Paste Transcript (for movies & podcasts)
Some videos (YouTube Movies, audio-only content) don't have accessible captions. Tap the 📋 icon next to Paste & Analyze. On YouTube, tap ··· → Open transcript → copy all → paste into ReelCall. Timestamps are stripped automatically.

Who: Search
Type a person's name — "Gromen", "Ferriss", "Jiang". Finds videos where that person appears regardless of which channel hosted them.
🏷️
Topic / About: Search
Type any subject — "crude oil", "anxiety protocol", "Bitcoin ETF". Searches across titles, summaries, key points, and quotes simultaneously.

Describe what you remember in plain language — "that video about the Norwegian exercise study" and AI finds the right video even if you don't know the title.

Grid vs List View
Toggle between Grid (thumbnail-first, 5 across on desktop) and List (title + 1-line summary preview). Grid is great for visual browsing. List is better for reading summaries before opening.

The Video Page
Everything you can do once a video is open
🔍
Credibility Check
Every video is automatically assessed for credibility when added. If concerns are found, a colored banner appears below the video header:
⚠️ Verify Before Sharing ⚠️ Credibility Concerns Detected
Tap 🔍 Deep Check for a full fact-checking analysis — specific claims are evaluated, missing context is flagged, and a verdict is returned. Results are saved so you never pay for the same check twice.
📤
Share a Video
Tap Share on any video. On iPhone and Android, your native share sheet opens — send directly via iMessage, WhatsApp, or any app. The recipient sees a clean summary page with key points and can ask questions. You earn +2 videos +7 days every time you share.
→
Key Points — Ask & Recall
Every key point has two buttons:
Ask → Pre-fills the Q&A input with "Tell me more about: [point]" and scrolls to the ask box. One tap to go deeper on anything.
☆ Recall Stars the point and highlights it in amber. Use this for insights worth remembering. Starred points show a badge on the video card in your library.
💬
Ask Questions
The Q&A section uses the full transcript — not just the summary. Ask anything about the video and get a specific, sourced answer. A brief loading bar appears while the transcript prepares. Your questions and answers are saved automatically so you can continue any session later.
💡 Example questions: "What specific exercise protocol did he recommend?" · "At what point did they mention the 40% statistic?" · "What's the counterargument they didn't address?"
🗨️
Discussion
Every video has a discussion section where ReelCall users can leave comments. If someone shares a video with you and you sign in, you'll see what others thought about it. Comments are visible to all logged-in users who have the video — a great way to share context with people you send videos to.
💡
Pro tip: Use Capture to Brain at the bottom of the video page to save personal notes or action items directly to your Studio Strong brain system — separate from the video's Q&A history.
🔬
Research Mode
Ask one question across multiple videos simultaneously
🔬
Research Mode is unlocked when you have 2+ videos. Find it in the top-right corner of your library. It lets you select any combination of videos and ask one question that gets answered using all of them at once — perfect for comparing perspectives, finding patterns, or researching a topic across multiple creators.
1
Type your question at the top
Enter what you want to know — "What do all these creators say about inflation?" or "Who disagrees with the consensus view?" You can type your question before selecting videos.
2
Filter to find the right videos
Use the Who: and About: boxes to filter which videos appear. Type "Gromen" to show only Luke Gromen videos. Type "gold" to show only gold-related content.
3
Select the videos to include
Click thumbnails to select them (up to 20). A checkmark appears on selected videos. Or use Select shown to grab all filtered results at once.
4
Ask — then ask more
Tap Ask. The answer synthesizes across all selected videos. After the first answer, the button changes to Ask More — each follow-up builds on previous context for a true research conversation.
Sessions are saved automatically. Find any previous Research Mode session in the Q&A History tab.
📊
Compare perspectives
"What does each creator say about the Fed's next move?" — get a side-by-side synthesis from all your finance videos at once.
🔗
Find connections
"Which of these videos reference the same study or data point?" — surface patterns and cross-references you'd never find manually.
⚡
Smart Find (paid)
Type a description in the search boxes and tap ✦ Smart Find — AI automatically selects the most relevant videos for your research question.

Sharing Videos
Share insights and earn rewards
1
Tap Share on any video
Open any video in your library and tap the Share button. On mobile, your native share sheet opens instantly — send to iMessage, WhatsApp, email, or any app.
2
Recipient sees a clean summary page
They get a full read-only view: thumbnail, summary, key points, key quotes, credibility assessment, and a working Q&A section. No account needed to read or ask questions.
3
You earn rewards every time
Every unique video you share earns you +2 videos and +7 days on your membership — unlimited. If they sign up from your shared link, you earn +15 videos and +15 days.
⚠️
Check credibility before sharing. The credibility banner appears automatically when concerns are detected. If you see an amber or red banner, run a Deep Check before sending to others.
