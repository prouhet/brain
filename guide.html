Getting Started Start here

ReelCall turns YouTube videos into a personal, searchable knowledge base. Instead of rewatching a video to find that one insight, you ask it a question.

1

Create your free account

Sign up with email and password. You start with 10 free videos — no credit card needed.

2

Add your first video

Paste any YouTube URL into the search box on the main page and click Analyze. ReelCall fetches the real transcript and Claude analyzes it in 10-30 seconds.

3

Ask it questions

Open any video and ask anything about it. Your Q&A is saved automatically so you can come back to it anytime.

4

Share what you find

Share any video with anyone — they see the summary and can ask questions. You earn +2 videos and +7 days membership for each share.

▶

Adding Videos

Three ways to add content to your library.

🔗 Paste a YouTube URL

Paste any YouTube link into the box on the main page. Supports standard links, short links (youtu.be), and share links with tracking parameters — they all work the same.

📺 Channel Import

Paste a channel URL (like youtube.com/@ChannelName or just @ChannelName) and click Browse Channel. ReelCall shows the 10 most recent videos as thumbnails. Select which ones to import and hit Import — they analyze one by one with a progress indicator.

  • Load 10 more to see older videos
  • Already-imported videos show greyed out so you don't duplicate
  • Great for catching up on a channel you follow

📋 Paste Transcript

If a video has no captions or you have text from another source, use the 📋 Paste button. Copy the transcript text from YouTube (three dots on video → Show transcript) and paste it in.

Longer videos get more key points automatically — a 2-hour video gets up to 18 key points while a 10-minute video gets 5. The analysis scales to the content.
💬

Q&A — Talk to Your Videos

Every video in your library can answer questions. Your Q&A history is saved automatically.

How it works

When you ask a question inside a video, Claude reads the full original transcript — not a summary. This means it can answer specific questions accurately, including details that might not have made it into the key points.

  • Ask about specific timestamps, claims, or recommendations
  • Follow-up questions continue the conversation
  • Every exchange is saved — come back to it anytime
  • Previous Q&A sessions are in the Q&A History tab

Q&A History tab

The Q&A History tab on the main library page shows all your saved Q&A sessions — both single-video and cross-query. Click any session to expand it and continue asking questions.

Your membership includes Q&A access. Each share you create adds +7 days to your membership timer.
✦

Cross-Query — Ask Across Videos

Cross-Query lets you ask one question and get answers synthesized from multiple videos simultaneously.

How it works

Cross-Query uses the summary and key points from each selected video — not the full transcripts. This means it's great for finding patterns, themes, and disagreements across your library. For specific details, use the individual video Q&A.

  • Click ✦ Cross-Query from the main library page
  • Select up to 20 videos (default is none — you choose)
  • Filter by channel or tag to find videos faster
  • Ask anything — Claude identifies the relevant videos and synthesizes
  • Sessions are saved to Q&A History automatically
  • Expand any saved session to continue the conversation

Best uses for Cross-Query

  • "What do all my FFTT videos say about the dollar?"
  • "What are the common themes across my finance videos?"
  • "Which creators disagree with each other and on what?"
  • "What action items appear most frequently?"
  • "Summarize everything I know about crude oil from my library"
20 videos is the sweet spot. Beyond that answers get broad. For specific channel analysis, filter to that channel first.

Sharing

Share any video with anyone — they get a clean, read-only view with the full summary, key points, and Q&A. No account required to view.

How to share

  • Open any video in your library
  • Click the Share button in the top right
  • A 🔗 Copy Link button appears — copy and send it to anyone
  • The recipient sees the full analysis and can ask questions
  • Click ✕ to stop sharing that video

What shared viewers see

  • Full video thumbnail and title
  • Complete summary and key points
  • Tags
  • Q&A — they can ask questions (ephemeral unless they save to their library)
  • Option to save to their own ReelCall library

🆕 What you earn from sharing

  • Each share you create: +2 videos +7 days membership
  • When someone signs up from your share: +15 videos +15 days membership
  • No cap on sharing rewards — the more you share, the more you earn
Sharing is the single most powerful thing you can do in ReelCall. Share a video once a week and your membership effectively never expires.

Your Stats

Track your library size, membership status, and sharing impact from the library page.

  • Days membership remaining — your current membership window, also with a progress bar

Pro Tips

🌙 Dark mode

Toggle between light and dark mode using the moon/sun icon in the library header. Your preference is saved automatically.

⭐ Star key points

Inside any video, star the key points worth recalling later. Starred points are highlighted in amber. The ★ badge on library cards shows how many starred points each video has.

🔍 Search your library

The search box on the main library page searches across titles, channels, summaries, tags, and Q&A content. If a match is found in your Q&A history, the card shows a 💬 Q&A match badge.

← Back button

The browser back button works within ReelCall — it always takes you back to the library without leaving the app. There's also a Back to Library button at the top and bottom of every video page.

📋 Paste transcript fallback

If a video shows "no captions available", try the 📋 Paste button. Go to YouTube, click the three dots on the video, select "Show transcript", copy it all, and paste it in.

💬 Save a shared video to your library

When someone shares a ReelCall video with you, you can save it to your own library with one click. Any Q&A you did on the shared page saves with it.
